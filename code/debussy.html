Claude Debussy was born on August 22, 1862, in Saint-Germain-en-Laye, France, to a family that struggled to make ends meet. His father's china shop and mother's seamstress business failed, and the family moved to Paris in 1864. Debussy's early life was marked by instability and financial struggles, but he discovered his passion for music at the age of 7 while taking piano lessons in Cannes during the Franco-Prussian War. He continued his studies at the Conservatoire de Paris, where he made rapid progress initially, but became increasingly undisciplined and careless in his studies. Despite his struggles, Debussy won several awards for his performances, including the premier accessit in 1875 and second prize in 1877.
